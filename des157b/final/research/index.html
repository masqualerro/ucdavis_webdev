Intergenerational Continuity of Taste: Parental and Adolescent Music Preferences

Tom F.M. ter Bogt, Marc J.M.H. Delsing, Maarten van Zalk, Peter G. Christenson and Wim H.J. Meeus

link pdf

This essay explores the “Intergenerational Continuity of Taste” in relation to music and families – how the musical taste of parents can be passed down or lost on their children. The essay also examines the structure of music taste itself and music I relation to gender, class etc. This essay is based on a survey with multiple test subjects, which will inspire me in how I choose to approach my data collection process for my project.

Cultural Heritage

ScienceDirect

link

This article gathers multiple outside sources to supplement their definition of "Culturual Heritage:"

Cultural Heritage is an expression of the ways of living developed by a community and passed on from generation to generation, including customs, practices, places, objects, artistic expressions, and values.

I am interested in exploring how music taste and personal art taste fits into Cultural Heritage, as I feel like it is incredibly relevant in a time where art is endlessly accessible.

What is Cultural Heritage?

Khan Academy

link

This article provides more definitions of "Cultural Heritage." There is one section in particular on the difference between tangible and intangible cultural heritage -- material objects and artifacts and concepts, tastes, traditions, ideas.
This is fascinating to me because the passing of art to new generations is both intagible and tangible -- tangible items are the conduit for the sharing of art while the concept of developing taste and knowledge is intagible.
